Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how the mobile application Luqua (the "App"), developed by Luong ("we", "us", or "our"), handles information when you use the App. The current version of Luqua is built around a simple principle: your data stays on your device. Please read this policy carefully to understand our practices, including how they may change if we add optional features such as advertising or paid features in the future (see Section 9).

1. Summary

  • The current version of Luqua does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal data with us or any third party.
  • Luqua does not require account creation, sign-in, or login of any kind.
  • The current version of Luqua does not connect to the internet to send your data anywhere and functions fully offline. We may add optional features (such as advertising or paid features) in future versions that require an internet connection — see Section 9.
  • All data generated by your use of the App (workout history, statistics, and settings) is stored locally on your device only.
  • Camera images used for pose detection are processed entirely on-device and are never recorded, saved, uploaded, or viewed by us.
  • You can permanently delete all locally stored data at any time from within the App.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

We do not collect any of the following, whether directly or through third-party services:

  • Name, email address, phone number, or any account/profile information (the App has no accounts)
  • Location data
  • Photos, videos, or audio recordings
  • Device identifiers, advertising IDs, or analytics/usage data
  • Crash logs sent to external services

The current version of Luqua does not integrate any advertising networks, analytics SDKs, crash-reporting services, or cloud/backend platforms (such as Firebase or Supabase). The current version contains no networking code and makes no network requests. If this changes in a future version, we will update this policy before the new feature is released — see Section 9.

3. Camera Use and On-Device Pose Detection

Luqua uses your device's camera to analyze your body posture in real time and provide exercise form feedback and repetition counts. This is powered by Google's ML Kit Pose Detection running entirely on your device.

  • Camera frames are processed in memory, in real time, and are immediately discarded.
  • No image, video, or frame is ever saved to your device's storage, your photo library, or any remote server.
  • No camera data ever leaves your device, including to us or to Google.
  • You may deny or revoke camera permission at any time in your device's system settings; doing so will only prevent pose-detection features from working.

4. Information Stored Locally On Your Device

To provide the App's core functionality, Luqua stores the following data locally on your device, using a local database (SQLite) and local app preferences. This information is never transmitted off your device:

DataPurposeStorage location
Workout sessions and exercise historyTrack your progress, repetitions, and statistics over timeLocal on-device database
App settings and preferencesRemember your chosen settings (e.g., language, notification preferences, display options)Local on-device storage
Scheduled remindersShow local notifications you have enabled (e.g., workout reminders)Local on-device scheduling, no push notification service

This data is associated only with your device. We have no access to it, no copy of it, and no way to identify you from it.

5. How to Delete Your Data

Because all of your data is stored only on your device, you are always in full control of it:

  • In-app deletion: Open Luqua, go to Settings → Data → Delete user data. After confirming, this permanently and immediately erases all workout history, statistics, and settings stored by the App. This action cannot be undone.
  • Uninstalling the App: Uninstalling Luqua from your device removes the App and all of its locally stored data.

6. Children's Privacy

The current version of Luqua does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children, because the App does not collect personal information from any user at all. Should we introduce features that collect data in the future (see Section 9), we will not knowingly collect personal information from children under the minimum age required by applicable law in their jurisdiction — for example, under 13 in the United States, or under the relevant minimum age (up to 16) in the European Economic Area — and we will handle any such features in compliance with applicable children's privacy laws.

7. Third-Party Services

Luqua uses Google's ML Kit library for on-device pose detection. ML Kit's on-device APIs run locally and do not send camera images or pose data to Google's servers. The App does not use any other third-party SDKs that process personal data.

8. International Users

Because the current version of Luqua does not collect, store, or transfer any personal data to us or any third party, there is no cross-border transfer of personal data in connection with your use of the App, regardless of your location.

9. Future Features (Advertising and Paid Features)

The current version of the App operates fully offline and does not collect or transmit any personal data, as described above. We may, however, introduce optional features in future versions — such as advertising or paid features (in-app purchases or subscriptions) — that require an internet connection and the use of third-party services, for example advertising networks, payment processors, or app store billing systems.

If and when we introduce any such feature:

  • We will update this Privacy Policy to describe what data is collected, by whom, and for what purpose, before the feature is released.
  • Any in-app purchases or subscriptions will be processed by the Apple App Store or Google Play; we do not receive or store your payment card details.
  • Advertising, if introduced, may involve third-party ad networks that collect limited data (such as device or advertising identifiers) under their own privacy policies. Where required by law, we will ask for your consent before such data is collected.
  • We will update the in-app data disclosure accordingly.

Until such features are released, the App remains fully offline as described in this policy.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time, for example to reflect new features or legal requirements. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. We encourage you to review this page periodically. Continued use of the App after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
